---
title: 'Brands now have to be legible to machines'
description: 'Reputation used to be built for people. Increasingly it is read by agents. That changes what a brand has to be.'
pubDate: 'Jun 16 2026'
---

For most of its history, a brand was something you built for people. The logo, the line, the way the phone got answered — all of it aimed at a human forming an impression.

That audience is no longer the only one that matters. More of the decisions about your brand are now made by machines acting on someone's behalf: which option an agent surfaces, which it skips, what it claims you stand for when asked. If a model can't read your brand cleanly, it will guess. And it will guess in the direction of whoever made themselves easiest to read.

This is not a creative problem. It is an operational one. The brands that win the next decade will be the ones that are legible — to people and to the systems increasingly standing between you and people. Consistent, machine-readable, unambiguous about what they are and, just as importantly, what they are not.

The work is less glamorous than a campaign and more consequential. It is infrastructure. And infrastructure is exactly the kind of thing that looks optional right up until the moment it isn't.
